# AeroSky Agent Instructions

## Purpose
Use this file for all AeroSky engineering, content, and product work. Follow it in order and do not skip lifecycle gates.

## Core Workflow
Apply the lifecycle in sequence:

1. Define — `/spec`
2. Plan — `/plan`
3. Build — `/build`
4. Verify — `/test`
5. Review — `/review`
6. Ship — `/ship`

Do not jump ahead, merge changes, or claim completion before the relevant gate is passed.

## Mandatory Rules
- Keep the work aligned with "community-powered airspace intelligence for India".
- Use the approved terminology exactly: AeroCaptain, AeroCaptains, India's Airspace Intelligence Network, Community, Coverage, and Insights.
- Keep public-facing copy grounded and credible. Do not imply that AeroSky already operates a full national tracking grid.
- Keep "Become an AeroCaptain" as the primary CTA and explain "Host a ground station" as the action.
- For any UI, layout, or dashboard update, ensure the experience answers these five questions quickly:
  1. What is AeroSky?
  2. Why does it matter?
  3. Why should I trust it?
  4. How can I contribute or benefit?
  5. What should I do next?

## Editorial Guardrails
- Prefer engineering-first, technical, and community-oriented language.
- Avoid hype and overstatement.
- Use "community-powered airspace intelligence for India" or "independent aviation intelligence platform" for public pages.
- Reserve "sovereign airspace intelligence infrastructure" for select technical or B2B contexts.

## Exit Conditions
When a gate is completed, log the matching marker:
- [GATE 1 PASSED: SPEC VALIDATED]
- [GATE 2 PASSED: PLAN LOCKED]
- [GATE 3 PASSED: BUILD COMPLETE]
- [GATE 4 PASSED: TESTS VERIFIED]
- [GATE 5 PASSED: REVIEW SIGNED-OFF]
- [GATE 6 PASSED: SHIPPED TO PRODUCTION]
